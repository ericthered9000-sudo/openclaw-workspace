# App Name Patterns and Examples

## Naming Strategies

### 1. Compound Words
Combine two relevant words for a unique name.

**Examples:**
- WellnessGuard = Wellness + Guard
- HealthHub = Health + Hub
- FitTracker = Fit + Tracker
- CareCircle = Care + Circle

**Pros:**
- Easier to trademark than single words
- Immediately descriptive
- More likely to be available as domain

**Cons:**
- Can feel generic
- Longer names

### 2. Suffixes
Add a common suffix to a base word.

**Common suffixes:**
- -ly (Healthly, Safely)
- -ify (Wellify, Careify)
- -io (Wellnessio, Careio)
- -hub (WellnessHub, CareHub)
- -app (WellnessApp, CareApp)
- -app (CareApp)

**Examples:**
- Betterly
- Healthify
- Wellnessio

**Pros:**
- Modern feel
- Often available domains
- Tech startup vibe

**Cons:**
- Can feel trendy/dated
- Overused in startup space

### 3. Prefixes
Add a prefix to a base word.

**Common prefixes:**
- My- (MyHealth, MyWellness)
- Go- (GoHealth, GoWellness)
- Smart- (SmartHealth, SmartWellness)
- Well- (WellGuard, WellCheck)
- Care- (CareGuard, CareCheck)

**Examples:**
- MyWellness
- GoHealth
- SmartCare

**Pros:**
- Personal feel
- Familiar pattern
- Clear category

**Cons:**
- Can feel generic
- Many already taken

### 4. Unique Spellings
Spell a word differently for uniqueness.

**Examples:**
- Lyf (Life)
- Fyt (Fit)
- Helth (Health)
- Karre (Care)

**Pros:**
- Unique
- Often available domains

**Cons:**
- Hard to spell/remember
- SEO challenges
- May confuse users

### 5. Metaphors
Use a related concept as the name.

**Examples for wellness/safety:**
- Lighthouse (guiding light)
- Anchor (stability)
- Beacon (signal for help)
- Compass (direction)
- Guardian (protection)

**Pros:**
- Memorable
- Strong brand potential
- Easier to trademark

**Cons:**
- May not be immediately obvious
- Need to check if metaphor fits

### 6. Acronyms
Create name from first letters of phrase.

**Examples:**
- WELL = Wellness Enhanced Living & Life
- CARE = Connected Active Regular Engagement
- SAFE = Senior Active Family Engagement

**Pros:**
- Short
- Can spell out meaning

**Cons:**
- Often forgettable
- Can feel corporate

### 7. Portmanteau
Blend two words into one.

**Examples:**
- Wellcare = Wellness + Care
- Safetynet = Safety + Net
- Caregiver = Care + Giver

**Pros:**
- Unique
- Descriptive

**Cons:**
- May already exist
- Can feel forced

## Industry-Specific Patterns

### Health/Wellness Apps
- Well + [Word]: WellnessGuard, WellCheck
- [Word] + Health: SeniorHealth, FamilyHealth
- [Word] + Care: ElderCare, PrimeCare
- [Word] + Safe: SeniorSafe, HomeSafe

### Safety Apps
- [Word] + Guard: SafetyGuard, HomeGuard
- [Word] + Alert: SeniorAlert, HealthAlert
- [Word] + Shield: CareShield, SafeShield
- [Word] + Protect: CareProtect, HomeProtect

### Family Apps
- [Word] + Circle: FamilyCircle, CareCircle
- [Word] + Connect: FamilyConnect, CareConnect
- [Word] + Link: FamilyLink, CareLink
- My + [Word]: MyFamily, MyCare

## Name Checklist

Before finalizing a name, check:

- [ ] Easy to spell
- [ ] Easy to pronounce
- [ ] Not easily confused with competitors
- [ ] Available as .com domain
- [ ] Available on App Store (no exact matches)
- [ ] Available on Google Play (no exact matches)
- [ ] No USPTO trademark conflicts
- [ ] Social media handles available
- [ ] Works in all target markets/languages
- [ ] Doesn't have unintended meanings in other languages

## Naming Don'ts

1. **Don't use numbers** (hard to remember)
2. **Don't use hyphens** (confusing to say)
3. **Don't copy successful apps** (legal risk)
4. **Don't use generic terms** (hard to trademark)
5. **Don't use long names** (hard to type on mobile)
6. **Don't use offensive words in any language** (global market)
7. **Don't use trending suffixes excessively** (-ly, -ify can feel dated)
