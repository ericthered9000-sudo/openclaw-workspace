### Retention Rates

| Period | Monthly Plans | Annual Plans |
|--------|---------------|--------------|
| 30 days | 40-60% | 60-80% |
| 90 days | 20-40% | 40-60% |
| 1 year | 10-15% | 25-35% |

**Key insight:** Annual plans retain 2-3x better than monthly.

## Pricing Strategies

### Freemium Model

**Best for:**
- Apps with clear free value
- Viral/growth potential
- Large user base strategy

**Typical split:**
- 95-97% free users
- 3-5% paid users

### Tiered Pricing

**Best for:**
- Apps with multiple user types (individual, team, enterprise)
- Clear feature differentiation

**Typical tiers:**
- Free
- Personal/Individual ($5-15/mo)
- Team/Family ($10-20/mo per user)
- Enterprise (custom)

### Usage-Based Pricing

**Best for:**
- API/service apps
- Storage-heavy apps
- Communication apps

**Typical model:**
- Free tier with limits
- Pay-per-use over limits
- Volume discounts

## Regional Pricing Considerations

### US Market
- Highest willingness to pay
- Annual discounts common (15-20% off)
- Premium positioning works

### EU Market
- Lower price sensitivity for B2B
- GDPR compliance required
- VAT included in pricing

### Emerging Markets
- Significantly lower pricing needed
- Local currency important
- Free tier more important

## Price Psychology

### Anchoring
- Show higher price first
- "Was $20, now $10" works
- Annual price shown as monthly equivalent

### Tier Positioning
- Most users pick middle tier
- Add "premium" tier to make mid-tier look better
- Free tier should have obvious limitations

### Discount Psychology
- 20% off feels like a "real" discount
- Annual = "2 months free" messaging
- Limited-time offers create urgency
